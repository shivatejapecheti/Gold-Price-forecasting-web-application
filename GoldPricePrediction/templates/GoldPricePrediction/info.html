What makes gold a precious metal?

Gold, the yellow metal, is considered rare and has higher economic value when compared with other metals. Gold metal has the biggest market of the five main precious metals traded in the market. It is referred to as the monetary metal owing to the history of its usage in currency. Apart from used as an ornament to enhance the beauty of women and men, the metal is a good conductor of electricity, highly malleable and is corrosion-resistant. Metal does not have an industrial component. It is the most sought form of investment during turbulent times. Metal has witnessed solid growth during the financial crisis period. Since the beginning of 2001, the gold price in the USA has witnessed a robust advancement of around 15 per cent every year.
